# Government & Law-Enforcement Data Request Policy

**Contact for requests:** privacy@xyrachat.com / legal@xyrachat.com
**Last updated:** 2026-06-27

This policy governs how Mll Nexus Group SL ("Xyra Chat", "we") handles requests
from public authorities (law enforcement, government agencies, courts, or
national-security bodies) for the personal data of users or of our customers'
end-users. It exists so our handling of such requests is lawful, minimal,
documented, and challengeable — and it backs the attestations we make to platform
partners (e.g. Meta).

## 1. Scope & roles
- For an end-customer's conversation data, **our business customer is the data
  controller and Xyra Chat is the data processor**. Where we receive a request
  about that data, we will, unless legally prohibited, **redirect the authority to
  the controller** and notify the affected customer so they can respond.
- For our own account/usage data (where we are the controller), we handle the
  request directly under this policy.

## 2. Required legality review
Every request is reviewed before any data is disclosed. We verify, at minimum:
- the request is **valid and binding** under applicable law and comes through
  proper legal channels (correct jurisdiction, authority, and instrument);
- the requesting authority is **legally entitled** to the data sought;
- the request is **specific** and not overbroad.
No data is disclosed on the basis of an informal or unverified request.

## 3. Challenging unlawful or overbroad requests
Where a request appears **unlawful, overbroad, vague, or improper**, we will
**push back, narrow, or formally challenge it** (including seeking to quash or
appeal) before disclosing anything, and we will seek legal advice as needed.

## 4. Data minimization
If disclosure is legally required, we disclose **only the minimum data strictly
necessary** to comply with the specific, valid request — never bulk or
"everything you have" disclosures. We scope the response to the named individuals,
time range, and data types the lawful order actually covers.

## 5. Documentation
We **log every request and our handling of it**, including: the requesting
authority and legal instrument, date received, the data sought, our legality
assessment and legal reasoning, who was involved, any challenge raised, and
exactly what (if anything) was disclosed and when. Records are retained per our
retention policy.

## 6. User notification
Where legally permitted, we **notify the affected user/customer** of a request
relating to their data before disclosure, so they can exercise their rights. We
suspend notification only where a valid legal order prohibits it.

## 7. National-security requests
National-security requests are handled under the same legality-review,
minimization, challenge, and documentation principles above, subject to any legal
restrictions on disclosure or notification.
